# Delhi Sanitation and Public Space Notes

Sanitation and public space issues typically involve Municipal Corporation of Delhi, New Delhi Municipal Council in NDMC areas, Delhi Development Authority for some parks and planned areas, market associations, and Resident Welfare Associations.

Relevant checks for sanitation and public space complaints:
- Identify whether the issue is missed collection, illegal dumping, construction debris, market waste, park maintenance, noise, or air pollution.
- Check land ownership and collection route responsibility.
- For market or high-footfall areas, coordinate with market associations and ward sanitation staff.
- For dumping near parks, schools, or water bodies, assess environmental and public health risk.
- Escalate repeated failures through public grievance systems with source evidence and photographs.

Planning recommendation pattern:
- Increase collection frequency or route enforcement for repeated waste accumulation.
- Add signage, bins, and enforcement for illegal dumping.
- Create a public reporting and follow-up loop with ward-level accountability.
