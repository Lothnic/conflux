# Delhi Drainage and Water Infrastructure Notes

Waterlogging, sewer overflow, and pipeline leaks may involve Delhi Jal Board, Public Works Department, Municipal Corporation of Delhi, Irrigation and Flood Control Department, and local disaster management authorities.

Relevant checks for water and drainage complaints:
- Determine whether the asset is sewer, drinking water pipeline, storm-water drain, roadside drain, or natural drain.
- Verify if complaints recur during rainfall or occur during dry weather.
- Check whether nearby hospitals, schools, transit corridors, or markets are affected.
- For waterlogging, inspect silted drains, blocked outfalls, damaged covers, road camber, and illegal dumping into drains.
- For sewage or contamination, prioritize public health risk and coordinate sampling where needed.

Planning recommendation pattern:
- Immediate inspection and desilting for blocked drains.
- Public health escalation for sewage overflow or drinking water contamination.
- Monsoon preparedness review for repeated waterlogging hotspots.
